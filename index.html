How to Make Salmon Onigiri

Published at 8:32 by Miya Osamu

Onigiri (おにぎり), sometimes called Omusubi (おむすび), are Japanese rice balls and very popular at Japanese convenience stores. In this recipe, you‘ll learn how to make onigiri using Salmon.

This Salmon Onigiri recipe combines lightly cured salmon with warm, fluffy rice, offering a simple yet authentic Japanese snack. The gentle curing and steaming process brings out the salmon’s natural richness while keeping it tender and moist. Wrapped in crisp nori, each rice ball delivers a satisfying balance of texture and flavor. It’s easy to prepare, making it perfect for quick meals, picnics, or packed lunches.

Whether you’re new to Japanese cooking or just craving something comforting, this onigiri is a delicious place to start.

Prep: 15 minutes
Cook: 5 minutes
Total: 20 minutes
Servings: 3 onigiri

Ingredients

  • ~200 g salmon fillet (preferably fatty so it stays moist)
  • ~5 gsalt (to dry-cure and season the salmon)
  • 2 tbsp sake (or water if you can’t get sake) for steaming the salmon
  • 2 cups warm, sticky short-grain rice
  • 2 sheets nori (cut into 3 strips) for wrapping
  • Extra salt as needed (optional, to season the outside of the rice)

Instructions

  1. Sprinkle salmon evenly with salt, place on paper towel or parchment, and refrigerate overnight to lightly cure it.
  2. Cook the salmon: place it skin-side down in a nonstick pan, pour in 2 tbsp sake, cover, bring to a boil, then lower the heat and steam for about 3 minutes.
  3. Let the salmon cool slightly, remove skin and bones, then shred the meat into flakes with your fingers.
  4. Prepare warm sticky rice. On a piece of plastic wrap, sprinkle a little salt, place some rice in the center, make a small indentation, add shredded salmon, then cover it with more rice.
  5. Shape the rice into a triangle with your hands—press gently so it holds together but stays soft.
  6. Wrap the bottom of the onigiri with a strip of nori. It’s best to add the nori right before eating so it stays crispy.

Notes & Tips

  1. Use fatty salmon so the filling stays tender and doesn’t dry out.
  2. Steaming the salmon with sake keeps the texture soft and adds umami.
  3. Short-grain/sushi rice is essential so the onigiri sticks together nicely.
  4. Don’t squeeze too hard when shaping—just firm enough to keep its shape.
  5. Add the nori right before eating so it doesn’t get soggy.

Enjoy your homemade Salmon Onigiri—simple, tasty, and always satisfying.
